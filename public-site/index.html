02The Principle

Precision Manifesto / Independent Analysis

Tools do not create precision.
A process does.

A tool may reveal structure, organize information, or help a trader interrogate an idea. It cannot decide whether the context is valid, the risk is acceptable, or the trader should participate.

That distinction is the useful lens for evaluating NextStepAlgo: education, indicators, AI guidance, and simulation are most valuable when they strengthen a repeatable process—not when they become a substitute for judgment.

03 / Three Decision Gates

Before any entry,
clear these gates.

Each Gate Produces a Specific Answer. “It Looks Good” Is Not One of Them.

01
Context

Explain the market before you trade it.

Where is price in the larger sequence? Which liquidity has been taken, and which remains relevant? If the context cannot be stated plainly, the setup is not yet precise.

02
Conditions

Define what must happen next.

A setup needs observable conditions—not a feeling. Write the confirmation, entry logic, and invalidation before the decision becomes emotional.

03
Risk

Know the failure point first.

Precision is incomplete without a predetermined loss condition. Position size, stop logic, and the decision to stand down belong in the plan.
